Problem Statement - Design a System Architecture of an Unmanned Aerial Vehicle.

You are tasked with building the system architecture of a multirotor. It has to be capable of carrying an Autopilot (flight controller), a Telemetry Radio, a GPS and any other devices you deem fit. The first step is to make a small test drone in which the flight controller, radios and software is checked. This vehicle has to be capable of sustaining a 20 min flight and reach a maximum height of 140 feet. Assume the mechanical assembly to be fit and robust.

Final Drone:

Now that the test drone has performed successfully, you are tasked with making a power supply for the real drone.

Submission - 3) Power Distribution Board for the Motors, Autopilot and Telemetry radio. The board is to be designed around the TI TPS5431 buck converter ICs (Information) to power the autopliot and radio.

To be submitted as a PCB in KiCAD or EAGLE( schematic and board layout) along with Bill of Materials (in excel format).

Design Requirements

  • The PCB should be capable of handling 100-150A continuously.
  • The high current path (to motors) and low current paths (to autopilot, radio etc) should be as isolated from each other as possible.
  • The motors and the input to the buck regulator should be at the same voltage.
  • Proper grounding techniques to be used.
  • A current sensor and voltage sensor capable of measuring maximum current and voltage to be included.
  • Compatible Connectors and Cables to be identified for each piece of hardware.
